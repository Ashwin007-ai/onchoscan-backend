FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know before your first scan.

No. OnchoScan is an AI-assisted research and educational screening tool. It is not a substitute for professional medical diagnosis. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional for clinical evaluation and treatment.
For brain tumor detection, upload MRI scans in JPEG, PNG, or TIFF format. For skin cancer screening, upload clear dermoscopic or standard photos of skin lesions. Higher resolution images generally produce better results.
Most analyses complete in under 60 seconds. The time includes image preprocessing, model inference, Grad-CAM heatmap generation, and PDF report creation. GPU-backed deployments may be faster.
OnchoScan uses fine-tuned ResNet-18 convolutional neural networks — one for brain tumor classification (4 classes) and one for skin cancer (2 classes). Grad-CAM is used for gradient-based visual explanation of predictions.
User accounts and prediction history are stored in a local SQLite database secured with JWT-based authentication and bcrypt password hashing. Images are processed server-side and not shared with third parties.
